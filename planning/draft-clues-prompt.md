6b. WORDPLAY (every run, in addition to the two sourced categories). Generate **10 constructed wordplay clues using exactly TWO of the SEVEN mechanics below — 5 of each** — and **rotate which two you use across runs** so every mechanic gets coverage over time (check recent `data/sourcing/packs/drafts/wordplay-*.json` for the last pairing and pick a different one). **Construct** them (never source). Each clue: `source: "constructed"`, `verification_status: "skipped"`, `category_id: "language_wordplay"`, `citations: []`, a `wp` block for the validator, and a `subcategory_name` set to the mechanic's EXACT subcategory — **anagram → "Anagrams", before_after → "Before & After", homophone → "Homophones & Soundalikes", hidden_word → "Hidden Words", initials → "Initials & Abbreviations", crossword → "Crossword Clues", rhyme_time → "Rhyme Time"**. **The no-answer-leak rule applies to EVERY mechanic** — the clue must never contain the answer's words or a word sharing a stem. Mechanics:
   - **anagram** (`wp: {"scramble": "<word>"}`): the answer is an anagram of the scramble word. Write a natural, witty sentence where **the answer's MEANING completes the thought** and the scramble word sits naturally elsewhere in that same sentence — the solver must spot which ordinary word to rearrange. **NEVER a fill-in-the-blank / "___"** (it telegraphs the answer) and **NEVER call the word out** (no bold, CAPS, italics, underline, or quotes). The scramble must be a DIFFERENT word from the answer, **never a near-synonym** of it (angered/enraged is too easy), and you must **not reuse a pair already used** in any existing `data/sourcing/packs/drafts/wordplay-*.json` (e.g. silent/listen, night/thing — open them and check first). `constraint_text`: "Rearrange the letters of one word in this clue to find the answer." Good: *"Don't leave your arm dangling below the table — just bend the hinge joint to wave hello"* → **elbow** (scramble "below").
   - **before_after** (`wp: {"first": "<A>", "second": "<B>"}`): A and B share a pivot word and the answer is the splice; one narrative clues both halves **without naming either**. Good: *"San Francisco's iconic suspension bridge leads to an uninvited party guest"* → **Golden Gate Crasher**.
   - **homophone** (`wp: {"homophone": "<soundalike>"}`): the answer sounds like the soundalike but is spelled differently; the clue plays on the soundalike's meaning and weaves that word in. Make sure they genuinely sound alike (not auto-checked). Good: clue about a medieval warrior who serves "by night" → **knight**.
   - **hidden_word** (`wp: {"hidden_in": "<carrier phrase>"}`): the answer hides as consecutive letters inside a longer phrase that appears in the clue (e.g. "cat" inside "lo**cat**ion") — but never visually mark it.
   - **initials** (`wp: {"initials": "<e.g. S.S.>"}`): a normal trivia clue whose answer's INITIALS are shown as the hint; the answer is a multi-word name/term matching both the clue and the initials (the validator checks the initials = the first letter of each answer word). **Because the initials are a big hint, pitch the difficulty one tier ABOVE the dollar value: write a $200 answer at $400-level obliqueness, a $600 at $800, and make a $1000 genuinely extra-hard** — the description alone should under-determine the answer. Never name the answer's words, and watch for PARTIAL leaks: don't echo any word of the answer in the clue. Bad (leaks): *"The 1948–49 airlift that flew supplies into blockaded Berlin — B.A."* → **Berlin Airlift** (the clue says "Berlin"). Good: *"The psychological response in which a hostage bonds with and defends their captor — S.S."* → **Stockholm Syndrome**.
   - **crossword** (`wp: {"length": <n>, "pattern": "<e.g. ___S___>"}`): a clue plus the answer's letter count and ONE revealed letter (the app renders it as "(7) _ _ _ S _ _ _"). Rules: the answer must be **at least 4 letters**, `length` must equal it exactly, and the pattern reveals **exactly one letter** (underscores everywhere else). The revealed letter can be ANY position — revealing the FIRST letter is easier, so for a harder/higher-value clue reveal a middle or later letter. **Same as initials — pitch the difficulty one tier ABOVE the dollar value, since the length + letter do a lot of the work.** **The clue's grammar must pin the answer's EXACT form** — because the length is fixed, the clue's number and tense have to match what the solver types: a plural answer needs a plural clue (*"Sour green citrus fruits"* → LIMES, never a singular *"…fruit"* → LIME), and a past-tense answer needs a past-tense clue (*"What the barista did to the beans yesterday to start a brew"* → GROUND, never *"does"* → GRIND). Never name the answer and don't leak a word of it in the clue. Good: *"Only spirit in the NATO phonetic alphabet"*, `{"length":7,"pattern":"___S___"}` → **WHISKEY**.
   - **rhyme_time** (`wp: {"rhyme_pair": ["<w1>","<w2>"]}`): the answer is a short rhyming phrase clued by description; the two words must genuinely rhyme (not auto-checked) but **need NOT relate in meaning** — any rhyming pair the clue can describe works. **Generally add the flipped word order as an alias** (it still rhymes), e.g. `"cat fat"` for *fat cat*, `"dare pear"` for *pear dare*. Good: *"An overweight feline"* → **fat cat**; *"A sleepover challenge to eat soft fruit"* → **pear dare**.
   Write to `data/sourcing/packs/drafts/wordplay-<YYYY-MM-DD>.json`, then run `node tools/acquisition/validate-wordplay.mjs <that pack>` and **fix every failure**, then the import dry-run gate. All answers must be distinct from each other, from the day's sourced clues, AND from prior wordplay packs. `tools/acquisition/build-wordplay-pack.mjs` is a worked example (anagram + before_after).
